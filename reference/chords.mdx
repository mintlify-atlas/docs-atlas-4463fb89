FretFlow's chord overlay supports 7 chord types. Chords are stored as arrays of semitone intervals from the chord root and computed using `getChordNotes(rootNote, chordName)` in `theory.ts`.

## Triads

### Major Triad

The foundation of Western harmony. Bright, stable, resolved.

**Intervals:** 1 – 3 – 5

**Semitones:** `[0, 4, 7]`

**Example (C Major Triad):** C E G

---

### Minor Triad

Dark and melancholic counterpart to the major triad.

**Intervals:** 1 – ♭3 – 5

**Semitones:** `[0, 3, 7]`

**Example (A Minor Triad):** A C E

---

### Diminished Triad

Tense and unstable. Common on the 7th degree of major scales.

**Intervals:** 1 – ♭3 – ♭5

**Semitones:** `[0, 3, 6]`

**Example (B Diminished):** B D F

---

## Seventh chords

### Major 7th

Lush, jazzy major chord with a leading-tone 7th. Common in jazz and neo-soul.

**Intervals:** 1 – 3 – 5 – 7

**Semitones:** `[0, 4, 7, 11]`

**Example (C Major 7th):** C E G B

---

### Minor 7th

Smooth minor chord with a minor 7th. The backbone of jazz and R&B.

**Intervals:** 1 – ♭3 – 5 – ♭7

**Semitones:** `[0, 3, 7, 10]`

**Example (A Minor 7th):** A C E G

---

### Dominant 7th

Major triad with a minor 7th. Creates tension that resolves a perfect fifth down. Essential in blues and jazz.

**Intervals:** 1 – 3 – 5 – ♭7

**Semitones:** `[0, 4, 7, 10]`

**Example (G Dominant 7th):** G B D F

---

## Other

### Power Chord (5)

Two-note chord — root and perfect 5th. No 3rd, so neither major nor minor in quality. Essential in rock and metal.

**Intervals:** 1 – 5

**Semitones:** `[0, 7]`

**Example (E5):** E B

<Note>
The chord overlay root can be set independently of the scale root. Uncheck **Link chord root to scale** to choose a different chord root from the 12-note grid.
</Note>
